    Emotional Intelligence

    Saturday, 2016-03-26

    The video had some very intriguing insights and information. The studies on the amygdala were particularly interesting, and the ideas presented around mindfulness and practicing to improve focus and attention were very compelling. Chade-Meng Tan seems a very likeable "jolly good fellow". His mention of the Navy study, among others, was also fascinating, although one thing I would have liked expansion on was how exactly the "good" officers were separated from the average ones. But his hypothesis makes perfect sense when you think about how important relationships with people are in any job (even "engineering/technical" ones), and in fact everyday life.

    It also seems that science disputes the theory given by nigahiga that "nice guys finish last". Nigahiga may not practice mindfulness and could benefit from training his emotional intelligence.

    Controlling one's emotions, a proponent of mindfulness presented in the video, is crucial in any job involving computers, as computers do not always do what you want them to do. Reacting to stressful situations in a calm manner is a great asset to have in software development. Specifically in web development, it can pay enormous dividends to be a zen in the face of frustration, since you'll most likely have to deal with the quirks of Javascript, along with an assortment of other technologies that may not be the most user-friendly at times.
