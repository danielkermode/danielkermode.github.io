Daily Mindfulness

Saturday, 2016-04-09

Conversation

I found the conversation with a friend a good experience. It was very worthwile. It can be helpful to pay attention to how much I am giving the other person the chance to speak in certain situations, as at times I can forget to acknowledge the other person and just spout out my own thoughts for too long, without realising that the other person may not be getting a chance to express themselves. Something which can extend any conversation (which was mentioned in the chapter) is to ask them questions, and then to make sure to listen and comprehend their viewpoint, as the outcome means you'll either hear an interesting new/opposite point of view or you'll find common ground that you can "circle jerk" over (a crude internet meme that means "to express approval of something with others").

The technique I found the most intriguing is also the laziest most efficient. I'm sure many programmers can appreciate this method. It involves taking just one mindful breath each day, and then your meditation commitment is fulfilled. I will echo Chade-Meng's wisdom here, since the chapter is very eloquently written:

This practice encourages you to generate an intention to do something kind and beneficial to yourself daily, and over time, that self-directed kindness becomes a valuable mental habit. When self-directed kindness is strong, mindfulness becomes easier.
Chade-Meng

Other Musings
