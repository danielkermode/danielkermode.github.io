    Process over Product

    Process over product is important when learning new concepts so as not to fall victim to a perfectionist mentality. For me, process over product is important for anything that involves visual design, since I'm certainly not the greatest artist and although I may want my site to look a lot more stylish and "polished", the functionality is more important and it's better for me to just go through the process of learning the way design is done so I can improve in this area. That being said, I'm not sure if I would always hold the mantra "process over product". For learning new things it can often be useful, but in other areas, product is in fact something to focus on, with the process by which that product is achieved still being important, but not necessarily paramount. Especially in software, there are often multiple ways to achieve the same desired result, each with their own advantages and disadvantages, and it is the end product that should be prioritised as this is ultimately the only thing the user really cares about.

    Meditation

    The idea of meditating for just 15 seconds a day is very interesting, and certainly something I haven't considered or tried. The mention of small steps to achieve large goals is something that seems to be a recurring theme, and is a powerful approach to consider especially when dealing with habits such as meditation.
