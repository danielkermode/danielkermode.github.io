    Time and Habits

    Tuesday, 2016-03-15

    The first couple of links talked about meditation and mindfulness. Meditation doesn't seem that interesting for me, a few of the links were too pseudosciencey for my liking and although it may be useful for many people, it doesn't tend to work for me. One of the lines stood out:

    Many people will tell you that allowing time and space to listen to your emotions, listen to your heart and just be mindful is either a waste of time or somehow ‘hippy psychobabble’.

    Many People

    I concur.

    The Pomodoro Technique I have come across before. The video is nice and catchy, but I'll be honest: I can't really understand exactly how it all works. I also don't own a tomato timer, so I think that this method may not be ideal for me.

    The article 'Making it big by starting small' I really liked, and found that a lot of it resonated with me. Small, incremental steps is how I ever accomplish anything.

    The video on habits also resonated with me. Chocolate sounds like a great motivator to get me to go to the gym, even if there is a slight canceling effect that occurs when the chocolate is consumed. It also presented the idea of small increments to build the foundations for a longer-lasting habit. It suggested that practice is not necessarily always fun; sometimes if you want to achieve a desired result, you must bite the bullet and do the hard yards. Of course, how hard the yards are will differ from person to person, and this idea was also suggested in the video with the notion that there are morning people and night people, which resonated with me because I am certainly not a morning person.
