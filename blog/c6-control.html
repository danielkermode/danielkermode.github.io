Control

Friday, 2016-04-22

Riding your emotions like a horse

The trend of "controlling one's emotions" continued this week with a chapter on self-regulation. The chapter mentioned controlling "triggers" and gave a small anecdote on "how not to strangle your mother-in-law". As an aside, why is it always mothers-in-law? You'd think that they bear some similarities in terms of personality to your beloved spouse, so why is there a stereotype surrounding this particular relation? Perhaps they have been unfairly branded. I haven't had a mother-in-law myself, though.

Making friends with emotions

I found the idea of treating emotions as friends very intriguing. Chade-Meng recommends to treat them with kindness even if they are negative emotions such as depression and anger. In doing this, perhaps the feelings will not come as often. A gentleman called Mingyur Rinpoche tried to make friends with panic. He had a panic disorder up until the age of 13, but found that after he made friends with the emotion, it went away. This made him sad, since he now missed his new friend.
